Program Review

An overview of the B.Eng. Mechanical Engineering program at FUTO: intended outcomes, structure, and graduate opportunities.

The B.Eng. Mechanical Engineering curriculum at FUTO prepares students with strong fundamentals in mathematics, physics, design, manufacturing, fluids, thermodynamics and systems engineering. The program emphasizes hands-on laboratory work, workshop skills, industrial attachment (SIWES), and a final-year project to develop professional competence and problem-solving abilities.

Program length: 5 years (including industrial training) — Accreditation: National Universities Commission (subject to change).

Entry Requirements

UTME / Direct Entry

  • Minimum UTME score as set by FUTO for the year
  • Five O'level credits including Mathematics, English, Physics, Chemistry, and any Technical subject.
  • Direct entry: HND (relevant) or two A-level passes in Physics and Mathematics.

Other Considerations

  • Post-UTME screening and interview (if applicable).
  • Evidence of mathematics proficiency recommended (additional remedial support provided where needed).
  • International applicants follow FUTO's international admissions policy.

Industrial Training (SIWES)

The Student Industrial Work Experience Scheme (SIWES) is a mandatory program for all Mechanical Engineering students at 400 level. This program provides students with practical experience in real engineering environments, bridging the gap between theoretical knowledge and industrial practice.

IT Duration

The industrial training spans a minimum of 6 months (one academic session), typically undertaken during the second semester of the 400 level academic year.

University Requirements

Students must complete all prerequisite courses up to 300 level before proceeding to SIWES. A SIWES coordinator is assigned to monitor student progress, and students are required to submit monthly reports and a final comprehensive report at the end of the program.

Placement Opportunities

Students can secure placements in various industries including:

  • Manufacturing companies (automotive, consumer goods, industrial equipment)
  • Oil and gas companies
  • Power generation and distribution companies
  • Construction and infrastructure firms
  • Research institutions and government agencies
  • Aviation and aerospace industries

Expected Skills

Upon completion, students should have gained practical experience in:

  • Engineering design and drafting using CAD software
  • Manufacturing processes and quality control
  • Equipment maintenance and troubleshooting
  • Project management and teamwork in engineering settings
  • Safety protocols and industrial regulations
  • Technical reporting and communication

Postgraduate Programs

Postgraduate Diploma (PGD)

12 Months (Full-time)

A conversion program for graduates from other disciplines or a refresher program for those seeking to update their knowledge in mechanical engineering.

Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree in any engineering or physical sciences
  • Minimum of third class honors or HND upper credit
  • NYSC discharge certificate

M.Sc. Mechanical Engineering

18 Months (Full-time)

Advanced study and research in specialized areas of mechanical engineering with options in thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, materials, or manufacturing.

Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering (minimum second class lower)
  • PGD in Mechanical Engineering (for non-mechanical graduates)
  • Research proposal in chosen specialization

Ph.D. Mechanical Engineering

36-48 Months (Full-time)

Doctoral research program for those seeking to contribute original knowledge to the field of mechanical engineering through advanced research.

Requirements

  • M.Sc. in Mechanical Engineering or related field
  • Strong research proposal
  • Publications in reputable journals (advantage)
